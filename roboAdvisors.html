Fields of Quantitative Finance

Before we dive into Robo Advisors, it is always good to take a quick glance at the fields of quantitative finance.

Quantitative finance is a field of applied mathematics, concerned with financial markets. Generally, mathematical finance will derive and extend the mathematical or numerical models without necessarily establishing a link to financial theory.

The application fields could be summarized as:

  • Asset Pricing Models
  • Portfolio Optimization
  • Fixed Income Securities
  • Derivatives Pricing
  • Asset and Liability Management
  • Capital Adequacy
  • Credit Risk Management
  • Extreme Value Theory

Application fields of Robo Advisors

Robo Advisors are online wealth management platforms that use technology to provide automated, algorithm-based portfolio management advice, limiting the need of human intervention for individuals.

Look closer on Robo Advisors

  • At the center of all robo advisors are algorithms such as Modern Portfolio Theory
  • An investor typically answers a series of questions about their finances, goals and risk tolerance before using the service
  • Fees for most robo-advisors range from free to 0.5% of assets, costs are 50-75% lower than their human counterparts
  • Typically offer only index funds and ETFs
  • Most don’t allow investments in individual stocks
  • Adopt a “passive” management style and aim to match the performance of a market index
  • Do not get involved in the more personal aspects of wealth management, such as taxes, insurance, retirement, estate planning or cash-flow management

So-called Modern Portfolio

  • Algorithm-based automated investment solutions to conduct portfolio management since at least 2005
  • So let’s look back to the application field of quantitative finance: Portfolio Optimization

Spec <- portfolioSpec()
